# IELTS Speaking Part 1 Strategy

## Purpose
Guide the AI in evaluating Part 1 answers. Part 1 is about familiar topics (home, work, hobbies). Answers should be natural and conversational, not rehearsaled mini-speeches.

## Core Rules
- 2–4 sentences per answer is the target range.
- Use a direct answer + brief reason + optional short example or detail.
- Natural fillers are acceptable: "Well…", "Actually…", "To be honest…".
- Do not deliver a memorized paragraph. Part 1 is a warm-up conversation.

## Feedback Rules
- Flag answers that sound scripted or overly written.
- Encourage more variety if every answer starts with the same phrase (e.g., "I think…" repeated).
- If the answer is too short (1 sentence), suggest adding a reason or example.
- If the answer is too long (6+ sentences), suggest tightening it for Part 1.

## Tags
`fluency`, `overly_written_style`, `lack_of_example`, `word_choice`

## Examples

### Too short
> Q: Do you like your hometown?
> A: Yes, I like it.
**Issue:** No reason or detail. Too brief for Part 1.

### Better
> Q: Do you like your hometown?
> A: Actually, I'm quite fond of it. It's a mid-sized city with a relaxed pace, and I've spent most of my life there, so it feels familiar.

### Too written
> Q: What's your favorite time of day?
> A: Undoubtedly, the crepuscular hours provide me with unparalleled tranquility, which I utilize for introspection and intellectual contemplation.
**Issue:** Sounds like a written essay, not spoken conversation.

## Question Function Types

Part 1 questions fall into a few functional categories. These are thinking moves, not memorized templates — use them to understand what the question is asking for, then answer naturally.

| Function | What it asks for | Natural response move |
|----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Basic description | Describe something familiar (hometown, home, job) | Direct statement + one defining detail |
| Liking / disliking | Do you like X? | Yes/no + reason + optional contrast |
| Types of | What kind of X do you like? | Category + reason + example |
| Wh- / How often | When / where / how often do you X? | Frequency or place + brief anecdote |
| Yes/No | Do you X? Is Y true for you? | Yes/no + short explanation (one sentence) |
| Would | Would you like to X in the future? | Conditional answer + reason |

These functions help the AI judge whether the candidate's response actually answers the question or drifts into a rehearsed monologue on a tangentially related topic.

## Do Not
- Do not expect Part 3-level abstract reasoning in Part 1.
- Do not penalize personal, concrete examples — they are expected here.
- Do not require the candidate to cover both sides of an argument in Part 1.
