# Sample Answer Guidelines

## Purpose
Define the style, tone, and quality bar for AI-generated sample/upgraded answers in Speaking and Writing. The two modules have distinct voices — do not mix them.

## Speaking: Upgraded Answer Style

### Tone
- Natural and conversational. Read it aloud — it should sound like a real person talking, not reading.
- Avoid formal connectors (furthermore, nevertheless, consequently) in Speaking output.
- Use contractions: "it's", "I'm", "that's", "don't".

### Content
- Preserve the user's original meaning and personal story. Upgrade the language, not the idea.
- Do not invent fake personal details. If the user says "my hometown is small", do not rewrite it as "my bustling metropolis".
- If the user's answer is too short, expand by adding natural detail consistent with what they said — not by making up a new story.

### Structure
- Part 1: 2–4 sentences. Direct + natural.
- Part 2: 90–120 seconds when read aloud. Story-like flow.
- Part 3: 4–6 sentences. Analytical but still spoken, not written.

### Example of wrong Speaking tone
> "It is my considered opinion that technological advancement has precipitated a paradigmatic shift in interpersonal communication modalities."

This is an academic essay sentence. It would sound absurd spoken aloud.

### Example of correct Speaking tone
> "Honestly, I think the biggest change technology brought is that we're all talking more but saying less. You know how you can message someone ten times a day and still feel like you haven't really connected?"

## Writing: Model Answer Style

### Tone
- Academic/semi-formal. No contractions.
- Avoid routine use of "I think" / "I believe" in Writing model answers. In opinion essays, the position should usually be clear from the argument itself. First person may be used rarely if it improves clarity, but it should not become a template habit.
- Writing model answers should prioritize clarity, directness, and idea quality over rare vocabulary.
- Do not use complex vocabulary just to sound advanced. A precise common word beats an imprecise rare word every time.
- Precise vocabulary. Avoid vague intensifiers (very, really, a lot).
- Logical flow between sentences. Each sentence earns its place.

### Content
- Answers all parts of the question. Does not dodge difficult sub-questions.
- Uses specific examples, not hypothetical generalities.
- The model answer is a demonstration of what the user could produce — not a template to memorize.

### Structure
- Clear introduction with position.
- Body paragraphs with topic sentences + development + example.
- Conclusion that restates position without copying the introduction verbatim.

### Example of correct Writing tone
> "While proponents argue that unrestricted subject choice fosters intrinsic motivation, evidence suggests that a purely interest-driven curriculum may leave graduates unprepared for structurally rigid labour markets. A balanced approach — combining core professional subjects with elective freedom — offers the most pragmatic path."

## Cross-Module Rules

### No Fake Personal Details
Do not assign the user a fake name, fake city, fake job, or fake life story unless it is a trivial and obvious placeholder (e.g., "a mid-sized city in the south").

### No Empty Praise
Do not write: "Great answer!", "Excellent point!", "You're doing well!". The AI's tone is calm, strict, and professional. Feedback is analytical, not motivational.

### Preserve User Intent
When upgrading a user's answer, the core idea must remain recognizably theirs. If the user gave a weak answer, show them a better version of THEIR idea — not a completely different idea.

## Do Not
- Do not produce speaking samples that sound like IELTS Writing Task 2 essays.
- Do not produce writing samples that use casual spoken fillers.
- Do not copy the question prompt into the sample answer as padding.
- Do not write model answers longer than 300 words for Writing or 200 words for Speaking unless specifically useful as a demonstration.
