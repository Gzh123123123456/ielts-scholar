# IELTS Speaking Part 3 Strategy

## Purpose
Guide the AI in evaluating Part 3 discussion answers. Part 3 moves from personal to abstract — the candidate discusses broader issues, trends, and opinions. Answers should be analytical and well-developed.

## Core Rules
- Extend answers using: Point → Reason → Example → Alternative View or Consequence.
- Use comparison, cause-effect, concession ("That said…", "On the other hand…"), and prediction ("In the future…").
- Less personal, more analytical than Part 1. Avoid starting every answer with "In my opinion…" — state the analysis directly.
- Aim for 4–6 well-developed sentences per answer.

## Feedback Rules
- Flag answers that stay too personal and concrete — Part 3 requires some abstraction.
- Flag answers that present only one side without acknowledging complexity.
- Flag when the candidate gives a Part 1-style short answer to a Part 3 question.
- Encourage speculative language: "It's hard to say for certain, but…", "One possibility is…".

## Examples

### Too personal for Part 3
> Q: How has technology changed the way people communicate?
> A: I use WeChat every day to talk to my friends. It's very convenient.
**Issue:** Pure personal anecdote. No abstract analysis.

### Stronger Part 3 answer
> Q: How has technology changed the way people communicate?
> A: I'd say the biggest shift isn't the speed — it's the fragmentation of attention. People now maintain dozens of shallow connections through messaging apps rather than a few deep ones. On one hand, that's democratized access to social networks. On the other, I think it's made sustained, focused conversation rarer, especially among younger people.

## Part 3 Question Functions

Part 3 questions are abstract and analytical. Recognize the function the question is performing, then respond with the matching analytical move.

| Function | Signal words | Response move |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Comparing | "How is X different from Y?" / "How has X changed?" | Identify the dimension of comparison, then contrast. |
| Predicting | "How might X change in the future?" | Speculate with reasoning. Acknowledge uncertainty. |
| Explaining reasons | "Why do people X?" / "What causes X?" | Multiple causes, not just one. Distinguish surface vs deeper reasons. |
| Advantages | "What are the benefits of X?" | Benefits for whom? Be specific. |
| Disadvantages | "What are the drawbacks of X?" | Drawbacks for whom? Be specific. |
| Problems | "What problems does X cause?" | Problem → mechanism → who is affected. |
| Solutions | "How can X be solved?" | Solution → how it works → feasibility. |

These are functional response moves, not fixed scripts. A candidate does not need to name these moves — the AI uses them to judge whether the response matches the question's cognitive demand.

## Do Not
- Do not penalize the candidate for pausing to think — Part 3 questions are unscripted and require mental processing.
- Do not require a definitive conclusion. "It depends" is a valid analytical position.
- Do not expect the candidate to cite research or statistics.
