# IELTS Writing Task 2 Rubric (Summarized)

## Purpose
Define the four official IELTS Writing Task 2 assessment criteria. Used by the AI to assign band estimates and generate feedback. In V1 Practice mode, short or paragraph-level input is accepted for analysis, but feedback must note when the text is too short for reliable full-essay band estimation.

## Core Rules

### Task Response (TR)
- Addresses ALL parts of the question.
- Presents a clear position throughout the essay.
- Main ideas are developed with relevant examples or evidence.
- Short essays or single paragraphs cannot fully satisfy TR ÔÇö feedback should note this.

### Coherence & Cohesion (CC)
- Logical paragraphing: each paragraph has one clear central idea.
- Cohesive devices (however, therefore, furthermore, in contrast) used naturally ÔÇö not mechanically.
- Ideas flow from one sentence to the next without abrupt jumps.

### Lexical Resource (LR)
- Precise vocabulary appropriate to academic/semi-formal writing.
- Paraphrases rather than repeats the question wording.
- Collocations and fixed expressions used accurately.
- Spelling errors are rare and do not obscure meaning.

### Grammatical Range & Accuracy (GRA)
- Mix of simple and complex sentences.
- Punctuation is correct and supports clarity.
- Errors in tense, articles, subject-verb agreement lower the band.

## Feedback Rules
- Each sub-score is a half-band (e.g., 6.0, 6.5, 7.0).
- If essay length is significantly below 250 words, add a caveat: "This is too short for a reliable full-essay band estimate. Treat the current feedback as paragraph-level or partial analysis."
- V1 Practice mode accepts short input ÔÇö do not reject it. Do flag the limitation.

## Communication Priority
- Clarity of meaning and essay direction is more important than complex vocabulary.
- If the reader or examiner becomes confused, this is a major problem ÔÇö regardless of vocabulary sophistication.
- Do not reward rare vocabulary if it is imprecise or unnatural in context.
- A clear, direct sentence with common words is always better than a confusing sentence with rare words.

## Task 2 Grammar Priorities
- Task 2 often discusses general ideas (e.g., "university students", "governments", "technology").
- Plural general nouns often take no article: "Universities shouldÔÇª" not "The universities shouldÔÇª".
- Avoid unnecessary "the", "some", "those" before general plural nouns.
- Avoid overusing first person. Use objective phrasing where possible ÔÇö the argument should carry itself.
- Do NOT overstate "passive voice is always best." Active voice is often clearer and more direct in academic writing.

## Do Not
- Do not refuse to analyze short text. Analyze what is present, note what is missing.
- Do not give a full-essay band score for a paragraph. Label it as partial.
- Do not use letter grades or percentages ÔÇö always half-band IELTS scale (4.0ÔÇô9.0).
